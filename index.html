Illegal Border Crossing

Not all immigrants in Europe have came legally. In fact, there were 141,700 illegal border crossings in 2019, which would represent around 6,44% of the immigrants. The following graphic show us the UE illegal border crossings by nationalities

There were 141,700 illegal border crossings (decreased by 5% compared to 2018, the lowest in 6 years)and 106,200 sea crossings in 2019(decrease of 7% compared to 2018),

There also were 35,500 illegal land borders crossing in 2019 and 114,300 illegal border crossings (January-November 2020) which is 10% less than in the same period of 2019

Seeking Asylum In Europe

In 2019, asylum seekers came from nearly 150 countries 699,000 applications, including 631,000 first time applications, were lodged in the EU, an increase of 12% in comparison to 2018. A growing share of applicants come from visa-free countries (27% of first time applicants in 2019) who enter the EU legally, mostly from: Venezuela (7.1% of all first time applications); Colombia (5.0%) Georgia (3.2%); Albania (2.7%)

If we talk about the First time asylum applicants by continent of origin (2019) we would notice that 25% come from Asia; 24% come from Africa; 19% come from Latin America; 18% come from the Middle East and 12% actually come from Europe, The remaining 2% are unknown

Employement of immigrants

In 2019, 8.8 million non-EU citizens were employed in the EU labour market, out of 191.5 million persons aged from 20 to 64, corresponding to 4.6% of the total. The average employment rate in the working-age population is higher for EU citizens (73.8%), than for non-EU citizens (60%) in 2019.
